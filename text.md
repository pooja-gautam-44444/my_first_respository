Moral Dilemma 1: The Misinformed Teacher
Verdict: The Court finds the Defendant Not Guilty, but recommends a "Correction of Procedure."

Reasoning: While the Prosecutor is correct that universalizing silence (Kantian) would lead to a world of unchallenged misinformation, the Defendant correctly identified the Golden Mean. Correcting a teacher mid-lecture on biological specifics when the core lesson is linguistic/philosophical (Panchkosh) risks the vice of Rashness. However, "Silence" is only the Mean if it is followed by a private correction. The "duty" of a science communicator is to the truth, but the "duty" of a student is to the learning environment.

Optimal Action: Private consultation post-class. This satisfies both Decorum and Truth.

Moral Dilemma 2: To Emigrate or Remain?
The Conflict: Patriotism/Communal Duty (Communitarianism) vs. Personal Flourishing (Virtue Ethics/Rational Egoism).

The Prosecution (Stay): You have a "Social Contract" with the society that raised you. Leaving is a "Brain Drain," harming the collective utility of your home nation.

The Defense (Leave): A person’s highest moral duty is to realize their full potential (Eudaimonia). If the current environment stifles your "Science Communicator" virtue, staying is a slow death of the self.

The Synthesis: If you can serve the world better from abroad (e.g., sending resources back or advancing global science), the Utilitarian value of leaving outweighs the local duty of staying.

Moral Dilemma 3: Teaching While Learning
The Conflict: The "Imposter" Risk (Deontology/Honesty) vs. The "Learning by Teaching" Benefit (Pragmatism).

The Prosecution: It is a "lie by omission" to present yourself as an authority when you are a student. You risk "poisoning the well" of the student's knowledge with half-baked facts.

The Defense: Docendo discimus (by teaching, we learn). If the teacher is transparent about being a "senior learner" rather than a "master," no contract is broken.

The Synthesis: Transparency is the moral pivot. If you disclose your status, the dilemma vanishes. It becomes a collaborative inquiry, which is a high-level educational virtue.

Moral Dilemma 4: Confronting Unprofessionalism
The Conflict: Integrity (Virtue Ethics) vs. Social Harmony/Self-Preservation (Prudence).

The Prosecution: Silence is complicity. By not confronting unprofessionalism, you normalize a "Lower Standard," which is a failure of Rule Utilitarianism.

The Defense: The "Cost-Benefit" analysis. If confrontation leads to career sabotage without changing the behavior, it is a Tactical Failure, not a moral victory.

The Synthesis: Use the Stoic Filter. Is the conduct within your "sphere of influence"? If yes, speak. If no, focus on your own excellence as a silent rebuke to their mediocrity.

Moral Dilemma 5: To Marry or Not
The Conflict: Traditional Duty/Human Connection (Natural Law) vs. Autonomy (Existentialism).

The Prosecution: Marriage is a vessel for long-term altruism and the "widening of the self" from "I" to "We."

The Defense: In an era where marriage is no longer a survival necessity, the Categorical Imperative cannot demand marriage, as a world of single people is not inherently contradictory.

The Synthesis: This is a Subjective Value choice. If marriage restricts your "flourishing" (Case 1's virtue theory), it is immoral for you. If it provides the stability to pursue your "science communicator" goals, it is a moral tool.

Moral Dilemma 6: The "To Be or Not To Be"
The Conflict: The Ultimate Question of Agency.

The Prosecution (Life): (Kantian) Suicide cannot be universalized; if everyone did it, there would be no moral agents left. (Utilitarian) It causes "Maximum Unhappiness" for the survivors.

The Defense: (Existentialist/Camus) Life is inherently absurd. The only truly serious philosophical problem is whether or not to live. Choosing to live in spite of the absurdity is the ultimate act of rebellion and freedom.

The Synthesis: As a Science Communicator, you are a "localized pocket of low entropy" in a decaying universe. Your existence allows the universe to observe itself. To cease is to delete a unique perspective of reality that can never be recovered.
