Moral dilemma 1 
“ Should i interrupt a Teacher who is teaching a correct preposition but backs it with a wrong facts ”



Case description
A teacher is teaching a preposition (panchkoshvikas) after explaining he/she tries to back scientifically but she/he said the “sugar is the cause of hepatitis, conjunctivitis and arthritis”
Therefore sugar is dangerous for anamykosha which leads to impaired learning. I identified the fact said about sugar is wrong (because of my science background) but stayed silent. 

Charges 
not fulfilling the duty as a science communicator and as student to correcting teacher if she/he makes a mistake

Case Proceedings

Prosecutor:- accused has failed to fulfil his duty as science communicator and as a student he should punished for that (kantian argument)

Defendant:- accused also have duty to maintain class decorum and respect the teacher so ought to this duty he avoid confrontation (kantian argument)

Prosecutor:- Accused have not chosen the best action that bring happiness in the world see Accused in his grief (positive utilitarian argument)

Defendant:- I have chosen the path that brings the least unhappiness. Because if I choose to confront that might be humiliating for the teacher, therefore more unhappiness. (Negative utilitarian argument)

Prosecutor:- A Straight lie! The accused has not chosen the path that brings the least unhappiness because if I extend his argument then the teacher will realise that she/he is wrong that intrigues her/him for exploring more on the topic therefore increasing the knowledge and joy. Not only that she/he will be proud that her/his student has critical thinking and spine to stand against authority if not wrong. Therefore more happiness and fellow batchmates of the accused will also be happy of him. (Negative utilitarian argument and Acquisition of perjury)

Defendant:- the calculations the Prosecutor made are quite elaborative which require time and at that moment I failed to do that and even the calculations you made are incomplete. so my calculations are the result of a lack of time. Hence my previous statement is not a lie. and by we have a rule to maintain classroom decorum (Criticism of utilitarian argument, defending the Acquisition of perjury and rule utilitarian argument)

Prosecutor [to accused]:- so you say that you have chosen the path of least unhappiness at restriction of time.

Defendent :- yes !

Prosecutor :- but how your argument justifies the morality of your action (criticism of naturalism: the open question argument)

Defendant :- what about rule of maintaining decorum (rule utilitarian argument)

Prosecutor :- you have the virtue of being courageous

Defendent :- yes!

Prosecutor :- your action doesn’t align with your virtue. Therefore you are not flourishing yourself. (Acquisition of immoral living in accordance of virtue theory)

Defendant :- The prosecutor has forgotten that every virtue is the mean of two extreme vices. In case of courage these vices are rashness and cowardice. Through the use of my practical wisdom I chose to stay silent to avoid being rash or rackless.(Golden Mean and Practical wisdom, defence for the charges Acquisition)

Prosecutor:- how will you prove that the mean value chosen by you is correct.

Defendant:- morals are relative as well as emotive. So it can’t be proven (moral relativism emotivism)

Prosecutor:- so according to you morals are relative which implies that each individual has the right to choose his own values ( individual morals ). If we assume that whatever the defendant said is correct then there is a paradox that is “how can validity of moral relativism be proven universally" because the core value of moral relativism itself won’t allow it. Hence the argument for justification of inaction stands baseless.The Prosecution RESTS!! (Criticism of moral relativism)

Defendent:- don’t forget emotivism prosecutor. Moral opinions are just emotional opinions there is nothing right or wrong in it.The defence REST!!
